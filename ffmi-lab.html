Academic Research: FFMI vs. Sarcopenia

Why BMI Fails the Masters Athlete

For the competitive athlete over 50, BMI is a misleading metric. It doesn't account for muscle density. FFMI provides a superior alternative by isolating lean muscle mass relative to height.

The Height Normalization Factor

Our lab uses the Normalized FFMI formula ($Final = FFMI + 6.1 \times (1.8 - Height)$). This ensures that taller and shorter athletes are compared on an even playing field, accounting for the natural scaling of skeletal muscle.

Longevity Benchmarks for Men (50+)

  • Below 18: Sarcopenia Risk
  • 18 - 20: Average Athlete
  • 20 - 22: High Performance
  • 22 - 25: Elite / Advanced

Note: Female athletes typically benchmark 2-3 points lower due to hormonal profiling and essential fat requirements.
