FFMI: The Sarcopenia Shield

Beyond BMI: BMI is a height-to-weight ratio that often misclassifies muscular masters athletes as "overweight." Fat-Free Mass Index (FFMI) isolates your muscle mass, providing a clearer picture of your structural reserve.

The Muscle Factor: After 50, muscle loss becomes a primary clinical concern. Maintaining a normalized FFMI above 19 (males) or 16 (females) is strongly associated with better metabolic health and functional independence.

Normalized Formula: We use the clinical standard: FFMI + 6.1 × (1.8 − height in meters). This ensures that athletes of all heights can accurately compare their muscle density against population norms.
