Academic Research: Insulin & The Masters Athlete

The Waist-to-Height Ratio (WtHR)

While BMI is often misleading for athletes with high muscle mass, WtHR is a direct proxy for visceral adiposity. Research in *The Lancet* suggests that keeping your waist circumference below half your height is the most significant predictor of insulin sensitivity and long-term metabolic health in men and women over 50.

Carbohydrate Tolerance

As athletes age, the "Anabolic Window" becomes more about nutrient partitioning than just calorie intake. High insulin sensitivity allows you to use carbohydrates to fuel glycolytic performance and recovery rather than storing them as adipose tissue.
