Academic Research: Autonomic Scaling for 50+

The HRV Baseline Shift

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is the most accurate non-invasive measure of the **Autonomic Nervous System**. For masters athletes, we don't look for the highest number; we look for the Stability of the Baseline. A sudden drop in HRV (rMSSD) indicates a sympathetic dominance, signaling that the Central Nervous System is still processing the previous load.

The Sleep Buffer

Sleep Architecture changes with age, often leading to less Deep (SWS) sleep. For the 50+ athlete, sleep is the primary "anabolic" phase where growth hormone is released to repair tissue. Short-changing sleep below 7 hours creates a **recovery debt** that no amount of supplementation can overcome.

Diagnostic Disclaimer: Recovery scores are proxies for physiological readiness. These results should be correlated with perceived exertion and morning mood. If you experience chronic fatigue, heart palpitations, or significant sleep disturbances, consult a physician to rule out overtraining syndrome or underlying medical conditions.
