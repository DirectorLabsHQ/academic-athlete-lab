Academic Research: Relative Power vs Absolute Watts

The Power-to-Weight Advantage

In cycling and rowing, absolute power is great for the flats, but **Watts per Kilogram (W/kg)** is what determines performance on a gradient. For the athlete over 50, defending your power-to-weight ratio often means focusing on fat loss and muscle retention, as increasing absolute power becomes physiologically more difficult after the fifth decade.

Masters Performance Benchmarks

A sustainble W/kg of **3.0+** is the standard for high-level amateur masters performance. Elite masters (Cat 1/2) often sustain **4.0 to 4.5 W/kg**. Tracking this metric allows you to see if your training is improving your "engine" even if your total weight fluctuates.

Diagnostic Disclaimer: Watts/kg results are dependent on accurate power meter calibration and scale data. Functional Threshold Power (FTP) testing is an extremely high-intensity effort; ensure you have medical clearance for Zone 5/Max exertion training.
