Academic Research: Preventing Metabolic Drift

The Katch-McArdle Advantage

Most calculators use age and height (Mifflin-St Jeor), which penalizes older athletes. This lab utilizes the Katch-McArdle Formula ($BMR = 370 + (21.6 \times \text{LBM in kg})$). By focusing on Lean Body Mass (LBM), we recognize that a muscular 55-year-old athlete has significantly higher energy demands than their sedentary peer.

Understanding Metabolic Drift

Metabolic "slowdown" is largely a byproduct of **Sarcopenia** (muscle loss) rather than chronological age alone. By maintaining a high FFMI (Fat-Free Mass Index) and consistent Zone 2/Resistance training, masters athletes can effectively "drift" their metabolic floor upwards, allowing for better nutrient partitioning and body composition control.

Diagnostic Disclaimer: TDEE is an estimate. Individual variation in NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis) and gut microbiome can impact actual maintenance by ±15%. Use this as a baseline and adjust based on weekly scale trends.
