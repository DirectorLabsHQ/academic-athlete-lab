The Science of Cardiovascular Offset

RHR & Autonomic Health

Resting Heart Rate (RHR) is a window into the health of your autonomic nervous system. For athletes over 50, a lower RHR typically correlates with higher heart rate variability (HRV) and a significantly "younger" biological profile. Every 10 BPM reduction below 70 is associated with a marked decrease in biological load.

Intensity vs. Aging

HIIT volume is the primary driver of mitochondrial density and mitochondrial turnover. High-intensity minutes (Zone 4/5) stimulate pathways that actively slow telomere shortening and improve cardiovascular elasticities, effectively "turning back the clock" on aerobic decline.
