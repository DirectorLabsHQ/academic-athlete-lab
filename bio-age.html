Research Protocol: Autonomic Age Markers

RHR & Vagal Tone

Resting Heart Rate (RHR) is a window into the parasympathetic nervous system. For athletes over 50, a lower RHR is often correlated with higher Vagal Tone and a more resilient cardiovascular system. This offset assumes a baseline RHR of 65 BPM as the standard "Chronological Zero."

The HIIT Multiplier

Zone 4 and Zone 5 training stimulus forces mitochondrial biogenesis and improves stroke volume. Research indicates that just 25–30 minutes of high-intensity work per week can significantly reverse the age-related decline in VO2 Max, effectively "de-aging" the heart by 5–10 years relative to sedentary peers.
