Academic Overview: Hydration & The Aging Athlete

The Hypohydration Risk in Masters Athletes

As we age, total body water decreases and the **thirst mechanism** becomes less sensitive. Research indicates that by the time an athlete over 50 feels "thirsty," they have often already reached a 2% body mass deficit—the threshold where cognitive function and power output begin to degrade rapidly.

Precision Sodium Replacement

Sweat is more than water. It contains significant amounts of sodium, potassium, and magnesium. For masters endurance athletes, replacing water without electrolytes can lead to **Hyponatremia** (critically low blood sodium).

A standard athletic baseline for replacement is ~750mg of Sodium per liter of fluid lost, though high-intensity efforts in heat may require upwards of 1200mg.
