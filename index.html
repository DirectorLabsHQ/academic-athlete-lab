Intelligent Strength Assessment for Athletes 50+

The Science of 1RM Estimation: For the masters athlete, testing a true 1-rep max (1RM) often carries a risk-to-reward ratio that is simply too high. Our laboratory uses the Brzycki Algorithm, a clinically validated formula that allows you to calculate your maximal force capacity using submaximal loads (ideally between 3-8 repetitions).

Combating Sarcopenia: Age-related muscle loss, or sarcopenia, can be mitigated through progressive overload. By accurately tracking your 1RM, you can program your working sets at the specific intensities (70-85%) required to maintain lean body mass and bone mineral density.

Gender-Specific Benchmarking: Physiological strength ceilings differ between male and female athletes. By selecting your gender profile, you ensure that your data is ready to be analyzed against the correct longevity standards in the next stage of our diagnostic suite.

CNS Preservation: High-intensity training is taxing on the Central Nervous System. This tool helps you find your "top end" without the systemic fatigue associated with maximal attempts, ensuring you can stay consistent with your training streak week after week.
