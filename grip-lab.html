Academic Research: Why Grip Matters

A Proxy for Biological Age

Extensive longitudinal studies (including PURE and UK Biobank) have demonstrated that grip strength is a more accurate predictor of cardiovascular health and all-cause mortality than systolic blood pressure. It measures the integrity of the Neuromuscular Junction, which is the first system to degrade with age.

The "Failing" Threshold

For the athlete over 50, a "passing" score isn't enough. We track the **Grip-to-Mass Ratio**. If your grip strength falls into the "Average" sedentary range, it indicates a lack of neural recruitment that will eventually limit your 1RM and VO2 performance.

Male Athletic Standards (KG)

  • Elite: 54+ kg
  • High Performance: 46 - 53 kg
  • Optimal: 38 - 45 kg
  • At Risk (Sarcopenia): < 28 kg

Female athletes: Standards typically scale at 60-65% of male cohorts. High performance is 30+ kg.
