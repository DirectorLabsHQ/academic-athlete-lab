Strength as a Biomarker of Functional Longevity

The Relative Strength Delta

While Bio-Age tracks your internal cardiovascular efficiency, Functional Age measures your mechanical output. For athletes over 50, the ability to move high external loads relative to body mass is the ultimate defense against frailty and bone density loss.

This lab uses relative strength ratios (Weight ÷ Bodyweight) to calculate how your current performance compares to clinical standards across different age brackets.

Sarcopenic Defense

A functional age lower than your chronological age indicates high skeletal muscle integrity. This acts as a protective buffer for the central nervous system and ensures long-term independence.

Target Longevity Ratio:

Achieving a 1.25x Deadlift (Male) or 1.0x Deadlift (Female) places you in the optimal survivability bracket for all-cause mortality in the 50+ population.
