Longevity Science: Strength as a Biomarker

Biological vs. Chronological

In clinical aging research, Biological Age reflects the functional status of your physiological systems. For athletes over 50, the most critical predictor of longevity is not just VO2 Max, but Relative Strength.

Our diagnostic uses relative strength ratios (Weight Lifted ÷ Bodyweight) to calculate how your musculoskeletal density compares to the clinical averages of younger demographics. This is a primary defense against Sarcopenic Obesity.

The "Functional Floor"

Maintaining a strength age lower than your chronological age indicates high skeletal muscle integrity and bone density. This "Functional Floor" protects the central nervous system and metabolic rate as you progress through the masters athlete lifecycle.

By hitting the 1.25x Deadlift Ratio (male) or 1.0x Deadlift Ratio (female), you are statistically categorized into the highest survivability bracket for all-cause mortality in 50+ populations.
