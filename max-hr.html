Academic Research: The 220-Age Fallacy

The Tanaka Formula (All Sexes)

The standard 220 - Age formula was never intended for athletic populations. It significantly underestimates the max HR of fit individuals over 50. This lab utilizes the Tanaka Formula ($208 - (0.7 \times \text{Age})$), which is the gold standard for healthy, aging adults.

The Gulati Formula (Female Specific)

Research by Dr. Martha Gulati found that the Tanaka and traditional formulas often overestimate the cardiac ceiling for women. The Gulati Formula ($206 - (0.88 \times \text{Age})$) provides a more accurate, research-backed limit for female athletes to ensure cardiovascular safety during interval training.

Diagnostic Disclaimer: Calculated Max HR is a statistical estimate. Individual variation can be $\pm 12$ beats per minute. For high-intensity athletic training, a medically supervised stress test is the only way to determine absolute maximum heart rate. Consult a cardiologist before performing Zone 5 efforts.
