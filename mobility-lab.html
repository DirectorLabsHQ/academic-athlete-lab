Academic Research: Mobility vs Flexibility

The Joint-By-Joint Approach

As proposed by Gray Cook and Mike Boyle, the body is a stack of joints alternating between **stability and mobility**. For the athlete over 50, a loss of ankle mobility forces the knee (a stability joint) to compensate, leading to chronic inflammation and tissue failure. We prioritize fROM over static stretching.

Connective Tissue Aging

With age, tendons and ligaments lose their elastic properties. Mobility training provides the mechanical loading necessary to stimulate tenocyte activity and maintain the integrity of the extracellular matrix. Functional range is the prerequisite for all power development.
