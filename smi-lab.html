Academic Research: SMI vs Aging

What is SMI?

The Skeletal Muscle Index (SMI) measures your **Appendicular Lean Soft Tissue (ALST)** relative to the square of your height. Unlike BMI, which aggregates fat and muscle, SMI isolatest the functional engine of the body. For athletes over 50, SMI is the primary biomarker for longevity and "healthspan" prediction.

Sarcopenia Prevention

Sarcopenia is the age-related loss of muscle mass and quality. Clinical research suggests that for males, an SMI below $7.0 \text{ kg/m}^2$ (and $5.7 \text{ kg/m}^2$ for females) represents a critical threshold for physical disability. Masters athletes should aim for the "Academic Tier" ($>9.0$ for men, $>7.5$ for women) to ensure athletic longevity.

Diagnostic Disclaimer: This calculation uses the ALST estimation formula based on total lean mass. For clinical accuracy, a DEXA scan or Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis (BIA) is recommended. Consult a physician if you experience rapid muscle loss or unexplained weakness.
